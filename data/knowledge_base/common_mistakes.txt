# Common Mistakes in JEE Mathematics

## Algebra Mistakes

### Quadratic Equations
- MISTAKE: Forgetting to check discriminant before claiming real roots.
- MISTAKE: Losing a root when dividing both sides by a variable (e.g. dividing by x when x=0 is a solution).
- MISTAKE: Incorrectly applying Vieta's formulas — confusing sum and product signs.

### Logarithms
- MISTAKE: Applying log to negative numbers or zero.
- MISTAKE: log(a+b) ≠ log(a) + log(b). Logs distribute over multiplication, NOT addition.
- MISTAKE: Forgetting domain restrictions: log_a(x) requires x > 0 and a > 0, a ≠ 1.

### Sequences & Series
- MISTAKE: Confusing AP and GP formulas.
- MISTAKE: Using sum-to-infinity formula when |r| ≥ 1.
- MISTAKE: Off-by-one error in counting terms.

## Calculus Mistakes

### Differentiation
- MISTAKE: Forgetting the chain rule when differentiating composite functions.
- MISTAKE: d/dx[f(x)g(x)] ≠ f'(x)g'(x). Must use product rule.
- MISTAKE: Not simplifying before differentiating (missing easier approaches).

### Integration
- MISTAKE: Forgetting the constant of integration C in indefinite integrals.
- MISTAKE: Incorrect substitution — forgetting to change the limits in definite integrals.
- MISTAKE: Using integration by parts with wrong choice of u and dv.

### Limits
- MISTAKE: Directly substituting into indeterminate forms (0/0, ∞/∞) without applying L'Hôpital or algebraic manipulation.
- MISTAKE: Applying L'Hôpital's rule when the form is NOT indeterminate.

## Probability Mistakes
- MISTAKE: Confusing permutations and combinations.
- MISTAKE: Forgetting to consider complementary counting.
- MISTAKE: P(A ∪ B) ≠ P(A) + P(B) unless A and B are mutually exclusive.
- MISTAKE: Assuming independence without verification.

## Linear Algebra Mistakes
- MISTAKE: Matrix multiplication is NOT commutative: AB ≠ BA in general.
- MISTAKE: Forgetting that det(kA) = kⁿ det(A) for n×n matrix.
- MISTAKE: Assuming inverse exists without checking det ≠ 0.
- MISTAKE: Wrong cofactor signs in determinant expansion.

## General Mistakes
- Not reading the question carefully — missing constraints like "positive integers" or "x > 0".
- Not checking answers by substitution.
- Arithmetic errors in final computation.
- Not considering edge cases (x = 0, division by zero, etc.).
